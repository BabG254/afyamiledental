When Dental Emergencies Strike

Dental emergencies can happen at any time and often require immediate attention to relieve pain, prevent infection, and save teeth. Our emergency dental services are designed to provide quick, effective treatment when you need it most.

Common Dental Emergencies

Severe Toothache

Intense tooth pain that interferes with daily activities.

Broken/Chipped Tooth

Cracked, fractured, or completely broken teeth from trauma.

Knocked-Out Tooth

Tooth completely displaced from its socket due to injury.

Dental Abscess

Serious infection around the tooth root or gums.

Lost Filling/Crown

Dental restoration that has fallen out or broken.

Soft Tissue Injury

Cuts or injuries to lips, cheeks, tongue, or gums.

Emergency First Aid Tips

For Toothache:

  • • Rinse mouth with warm salt water
  • • Use dental floss to remove trapped food
  • • Apply cold compress to outside of cheek
  • • Take over-the-counter pain medication

For Knocked-Out Tooth:

  • • Handle tooth by crown, not root
  • • Rinse gently with water if dirty
  • • Try to reinsert in socket
  • • Keep moist in milk or saliva
  • • See dentist within 30 minutes
